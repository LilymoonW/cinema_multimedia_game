# Shadow Quest — A Quick Godot Game for my Cinema Creative Project

![Title screen](screenshots/title.png)

## Inspiration
In class we were talking about parables and allegories. We had an exercise to create a parable or allegory for our class, so I came up with a simple story that subverts expectations. I like playing on the idea of a user thinking they are the protagonist like the genre typically holds, and subverting that to make the user think about what they are doing. Think Black Mirror episodes and games like *Omori* and *Undertale*. I actually don't play many games myself, but I watch a lot of game streams!

Given that, I wanted the art to be very cute and adorable. I took a lot of inspo from online and finally created this colorful space.

## Walking through the world
![Walking tutorial](screenshots/walking.png)

I created a lot of the assets myself — the player, the sparkle companion, the trees, the corrupted blobs, and the dreamy backgrounds.

## The combat & the twist
![Battle scene](screenshots/battle.png)

There's a very basic attack mechanic where Pip — the sparkle character that acts as the player's companion — cheers you on to kill and "save the world," playing on the typical theme of games which is to kill and conquer for glory. However, if you take time to observe, you see that these blobs might not be so evil — hinted at by how easy they are to kill.

Pip continues to enable you and cheers you on as you gather *hope* by killing more "corrupted" ones.

The boss fight is this evil figure, but flashes of another appearance show through — which should indicate that what you see may not be what is actually occurring. The evil corrupted ones might not be so corrupted after all.

## Endings
There are a few endings to this short game, but the mechanics are pretty simple — it's just meant to be a fun little project!
